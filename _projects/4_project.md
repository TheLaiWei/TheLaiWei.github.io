To facilitate robust interpretation of high-dimensional optical biosensing data, I incorporate machine learning–assisted analytical frameworks to extract diagnostic information from fluorescence measurements. In the SWIFT-FISH platform, quantitative spectral and intensity features derived from fluorescence in situ hybridization signals are analyzed using supervised classification algorithms to enable accurate bacterial identification and phenotypic characterization. Using machine learning to assist the analysis of multiplexed fluorescence signatures, the system achieved greater than 99% classification accuracy across six different bacterial species, demonstrating the ability of data-driven approaches to enhance the speed, reliability, and scalability of optical diagnostic platforms.
