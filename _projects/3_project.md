During my undergraduate studies, I worked on the development of a handheld point-of-care diagnostic platform, CyBIS (Cytocapture of Biomarkers In-Situ), designed for rapid infection detection through microfluidic-enabled biomarker capture and optical sensing. This project, which received the George Washington University SEAS Pelton Award for Outstanding Senior Project, provided extensive experience in rapid prototyping, optical system miniaturization, and the integration of microfluidics with compact handheld instrumentation. Through this work, I developed practical skills in translating laboratory-scale diagnostic technologies into portable point-of-care devices suitable for real-world clinical environments.
